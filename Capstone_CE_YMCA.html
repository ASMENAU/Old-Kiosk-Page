The Flagstaff YMCA Educational Enrichment Pathway: A Pathway to Learning and Health
Team Members: William Cowan, William Orr, Yang Li, Reyburn Joe & Cameron Ragan
Abstract:
BBRCY Engineering utilized modern engineering techniques and analysis methods to design a walking trail for the Flagstaff YMCA. Thorough floodplain and stream flow analysis, extensive surveying, and soil study culminated in the alignment and design of a natural surface pathway. The trail is designed in compliance with City of Flagstaff (CoF) criteria and in accordance with the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) floodplain mapping. Future Flagstaff Urban Trail System (FUTS) developments can easily integrate the pathway via the specified FUTS compliant footbridge. Along its route, the trail includes numerous kiosks to educate America's youth on the impact of littering, the beauty of nature, and the responsibility they possess to protect it.
Details:
The surface of the trail may be up to 2 inches thick of 100% aggregate or earth ground without pavement. Though the trail will be designed specifically for younger users, and the direct needs of the Flagstaff YMCA; the trail can be tied into the existing Flagstaff Urban Trail System (FUTS) located approximately 100 feet from the project location. In addition, a pre-manufactured pedestrian bridge will be built connecting this nature trail to the existing FUTS trail, Switzer Canyon Trail.
