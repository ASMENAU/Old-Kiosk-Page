Green RO Pretreatment System
Team Members: Amanda Shapter, Brandon Curtis, Brandon Giardino, Chris Livingston, Marc Miller & Eric Toloskiewich
Abstract:
Our senior design team from the Environmental Engineering department developed a green pretreatment system for a reverse osmosis (RO) membrane on a sea vessel for the WERC competition. The new pretreatment system replaces the current use of cartridge filters with an environmentally sustainable solution. A bench scale model was constructed to demonstrate the system's ability to deliver seawater to the RO system with particle diameters of less than 0.1 micron. Using foam fractation and multimedia filtration, the pretreatment system will handle high levels of bio-solids and turbidity found in coastal waters. In addition, the new system will have the ability to function for four months without significant maintenance.
Project Specifications:
The sea vessels are looking to increase the life span of the filter to 4 months and lower the amount of storage space to 100 cubic feet. To prolong RO membrane life and achieve 40% RO recovery, the desired particle size before RO treatment is 0.1-microns. The system may not exceed 3 times greater than the current systems size and must use no more in energy than 10 percent of the RO process. The system must be able to handle 30,000 gallons per day (GPD). The new RO pretreatment process may also be interchangeable to on-land desalination plants and the concept of Go Green should be taken into consideration.
