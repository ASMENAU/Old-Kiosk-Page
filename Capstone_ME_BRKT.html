BRKT
Team Members: Ariff Muhidin, Dean Pierce, Dan Moan & Steven Cureton
Abstract:
The purpose of our project is to design a bracket and test teh bracket material for a TPS (Thermal Protection System) panel that will likely be a key component of future spacecraft. The panel that the brackets are designed for will be subjected to significant and combined thermal and/or mechanical strains. The bracket will be used to transfer panel loadings to structural supports. The material used for the bracket currently has unknown mechanical properties. BRKT is tasked with: designing a suitable method for testing the Alumina Paper Coated with Mullite (APCM) material, providing recommendations based on the information gathered, developing and potentially testing bracket prototypes, and finally detailing the results in a document to be delivered to SMARF and ultimately to NASA.
Problem Statement:
Through the last 60 years of development for spacecraft and rockets, several key aspects of their design have become critical components to the vehicles' successes. A few examples of these compenents are the propulsion system, the payload, tracking/ communications equipment, and the Thermal Protection System (TPS). The thermal protection system had a unique role of insuring the survival of the vehicle. The thermal protection system protects the vehicle from the extreme temperatures experienced at supersonic speeds or upon reentry into a planet's atmosphere throught the uses of advanced ceramic materials.
