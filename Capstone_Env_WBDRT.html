Warmhouse Beach Dump Remediation - Treatment Engineering Team
Team Members: Ben Greenwell, Lydell Yazzie, Michael Orman & Natalie Bedard
Problem Statement:
The Warmhouse Beach Open Dump, located at the northwestern tip of Washington State, has a long history of unauthorized dumping of hazardous materials and municipal solid waste. The site is up-gradient from a beach with recreational and cultural significance to the Makah Tribe. Closure of the dump will include consolidating and encapsulating the waste onsite following the completion of a transfer station. Analysis of samples gathered from the site reveal contaminants such as heavy metals, dioxins, and polychlorinated biphenyls. This project consists of the design of an on-site treatment system for contaminated landfill leachate that will be generated and collected post-closure of the landfill, meeting all regulatory requirements for treated effluents.
Process:
The team, in conjunction with Ridolfi Engineering, will identify the contaminants of concern and will evaluate the state of the art technologies to determine which will best meet the criteria. The team anticipates these criteria will focus on cost of implementation and maintenance, energy efficiency, and effectiveness of remediation. Once the identification of the contaminants of concern is complete, the team will design a complete treatment process capable of treating the water to the required wastewater quality standards established by the EPA.
