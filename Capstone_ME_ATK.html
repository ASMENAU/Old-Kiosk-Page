ATK Weapons Manufacturing
Team Members: Catherine Carter, Mark Crouse, Aaron Howerton & Jonathan Mulder
Abstract:
ATK is one of the largest aerospace and defense contractors in the world. They have a significant presence in the medium caliber armored vehicle armament segment. This project specifically pertains to the 30mm programmable air bursting munitions fired from the MK44 Bushmaster Cannon. These rounds are capable of traveling preset distances and exploding mid-flight. To do this, they require a programming connection to the system firing them. To test this, an inert test verification round was produced that gives a go/no-go indication of the connection between the round and the onboard computer.
Problem Statement:
Redesign the inert SVR (Setter Verification Round) that models a PABM (Programmable Air-Bursting Munitions) to withstand wear between battery contacts and electronics. The SVR must withstand cycling through the testing system in excess of 1000 times. In addition, it must be able to operate under numerous environmental conditions. Finally, the chosen design must be a CG match to a live PABM round.
