Biogeography of Microbial Communities
Team Members: Michael Dwan, Jai Rideout, Logan Knecht, Damien Coy & Levi McCracken
Abstract:
With the cost of DNA sequencing data rapidly decreasing, microbial ecology studies are increasing in scope and complexity; more samples are being taken across varying environmental gradients, making analysis even more difficult than in the past. Many gradient-analysis statistical methods have been used in traditional macro-scale ecology for years to address these types of data; however, they have not been properly evaluated on micro-scale ecological data to verify that biologically-meaningful results are obtained. To this end, Team GenCode will be researching, testing, and evaluating these statistical methods with both real and simulated data sets to determine their effectiveness.
Background:
Microbial community analysis is a growing area of biological research area and frequently the subject of high-profile articles in scientific and popular literature. This project will systematically evaluate a series of metrics for analyzing changes across gradients in microbial communities. These methods will first be implemented in the QIIME software package, and subsequently tested on both simulated microbial community data and a collection of real data generated on the 454 and Illumina sequencing platforms.
