Roatan Wastewater Treatment Design Project
Team Members: Arthur Fairley, James Goudreau, Cherise John & Nicholas Lewandowski
Problem Statement:
The purpose of this project was to provide a wastewater system for a grouping of 3-5 houses in Policarpo, Honduras. Most of the small Policarpo community does not use formal wastewater treatment systems, but rather disposes of their waste into open pits, if not directly discharging into the community. As a result of the poor sanitary conditions, citizens are constantly exposed to deadly bacteria and viruses.
Project Objective:
The design team analyzed field research data and current design technologies to aid them in the design of a septic tank paired with an evapotranspiration bed. This system was designed to enhance the community's way of life and the quality of the surface runoff discharging into the ocean. With this first step of creating a simple, cost effective treatment system for a small grouping of houses, the design team hopes to open avenues for a larger, centralized system that will serve the entire community.
