Nestle Purina On-site Water Recycling
Team Members: Clinton Chadwick, Clinton Chadwick, Taylor Domin & Sabree Garbagni
Abstract:
Currently, Nestle Purina® discharges approximately 6000 gallons a day as Reverse Osmosis (RO) concentrate to the sewer. The purpose of this capstone project was to conduct an investigation and feasibility study to produce a design matrix that will aid Nestle Purina® in choosing the most practicle design solution for recycling their RO concentrate. An in depth water quality analysis of the RO concentrate relating water quality to potential water recycling applications within the plant and tank, pipe, and pump sizing and layout throughout the plant was required to formulate solutions. Cost benefit analyses were conducted for implementation of each proposed design solution and were included in the feasibility study.
Details:
The final product will be an improvement upon Nestle Purina's current water distribution system, treatment process, and water usage efficiency. The design solution may include pipes, equalization tanks, pumps, valves, fittings, and bracing members. The final product has the potential to reuse approximately 5000 gallons per day.
