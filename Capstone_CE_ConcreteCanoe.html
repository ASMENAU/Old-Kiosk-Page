ASCE Concrete Canoe: Betula
Team Members: Lindsey Childers, Marlo Abramowitz, Cristina Behrens, Kristen Solleveld & Linda Waters
Abstract:
Every year the Northern Arizona University's (NAU) American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) Chapter participates in the Pacific Southwest Regional Conference. Teams are challenged to design and construct a concrete canoe that can be raced at the conference. Each team creates a unique concrete mix and hull design using technical knowledge and skill. Some of the tasks involved in the design and construction of a concrete canoe are: development of concrete mixes, material selection and acquisition, testing concrete mixes, building the canoe mold, structural analysis calculations, and pouring and finishing of the canoe. This year's team designed and built Betula. The compressive strength of the final mix is 1834 psi, the unit weight is 60.2 lbs/ft^3, and Betula's hull thickness is Â½ inch.
Details:
Design of concrete mix requires selecting aggregates, admixtures, and cementitious materials to formulate unique design mixes that satisfy rules and requirements as well as provide strong, lightweight concrete. Possible aggregates include: VCAS Pozzolan, Cenospheres, K1 Microspheres, and Poraver. Possible admixtures include: Water Reducer, Air Entrainer, and Superplasticizers.
